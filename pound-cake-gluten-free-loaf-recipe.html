A Note From My Kitchen

When I make this cake, I pay close attention during the creaming stage. Butter and sugar should look noticeably lighter in color and fluffier in texture before you move on. That extra few minutes helps create a more even crumb and gives the loaf a lovely rise.

Room-temperature eggs matter here more than people think. If the eggs are too cold, the batter can look a little curdled after each addition. It usually comes together once the dry ingredients go in, but starting with room-temperature ingredients makes the whole process smoother.

Because gluten-free flour blends can vary slightly, I like to stop mixing as soon as the batter looks evenly combined. Overworking the batter is not helpful, and a gentle hand keeps the loaf tender. The finished batter should be thick, smooth, and easy to spread into the pan.

If the top begins to brown too quickly in the oven, loosely tenting with foil halfway through is a smart move. That protects the crust while the center finishes baking, so you get a golden loaf that is fully set without becoming too dark on top.

Ingredient Notes and Easy Swaps:

This recipe uses a short list of familiar ingredients, and each one plays an important role in the final texture and flavor. Since this is a simple loaf, the quality and condition of the ingredients really do make a difference. Softened butter, room-temperature eggs, and a good gluten-free flour blend all help create a batter that mixes smoothly and bakes evenly. The flavor is buttery and classic, with vanilla adding warmth and optional citrus zest bringing brightness. If you need a few adjustments, there are also some easy swaps that still stay true to the recipe’s character.

  • Unsalted butter: Butter is the foundation of the loaf’s rich flavor and classic pound cake texture. Make sure it is softened so it creams properly with the sugar; if needed, plant-based butter can be used for a dairy-free version.
  • Granulated sugar: Sugar sweetens the cake and also helps with the light, fluffy creaming stage. It contributes to the tender crumb and the golden top, so it is best not to reduce it if you want the intended texture.
  • Eggs: Eggs add structure, richness, and moisture while helping the batter emulsify. Let them come to room temperature first so they blend in smoothly and keep the batter from separating.
  • Vanilla extract: Vanilla rounds out the buttery flavor and gives the loaf that familiar bakery-style aroma. Use a good-quality vanilla if you can, since the flavor is noticeable in a simple cake like this.
  • Gluten-free all-purpose flour blend with xanthan gum: This is essential for the cake’s structure and sliceable crumb. A blend that already contains xanthan gum is best here, because it helps replace the elasticity that gluten would normally provide.
  • Baking powder: A small amount gives the loaf a gentle lift without changing its classic dense character. Check that yours is fresh so the cake rises evenly and does not bake up heavy in the center.
  • Salt: Even a little salt makes a big difference by balancing the sweetness and sharpening the buttery notes. It helps the vanilla and optional citrus taste brighter too.
  • Milk: Milk loosens the batter just enough and adds moisture for a soft interior. Whole or 2% both work well, and non-dairy milk can be substituted if you are making the loaf dairy-free.
  • Lemon or orange zest: This optional addition gives the cake a fresh, fragrant lift without overpowering the classic flavor. It is especially nice if you plan to serve the loaf with fruit, whipped cream, or a simple glaze.
A 9×5-inch loaf pan is important for the correct shape and baking time. If you are making substitutions, keep the flour blend one that includes xanthan gum, and use a milk alternative with a neutral flavor for the best overall balance.

How to Make buttery moist gluten-free pound cake (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Start by heating your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9×5-inch loaf pan well, then line it with parchment so the cake lifts out easily after baking.

Step 2: In a large bowl, beat the softened butter and sugar until the mixture looks pale, fluffy, and airy. This should take about 3 to 5 minutes, and it sets the stage for a tender loaf.

Step 3: Add the eggs one at a time, mixing thoroughly after each one so the batter stays smooth. Stir in the vanilla, and if you like a brighter flavor, add the lemon or orange zest here too.

Step 4: In a separate bowl, combine the gluten-free flour blend, baking powder, and salt. Whisk until everything is evenly distributed so the dry ingredients mix into the batter without pockets.

Step 5: Add the dry mixture and milk to the wet ingredients in alternating additions, beginning and ending with the dry ingredients. Mix just until the batter comes together and looks thick, smooth, and fully incorporated.

Step 6: Transfer the batter to your prepared loaf pan and spread it evenly from corner to corner. Smooth the top so the loaf bakes up neatly and evenly.

Step 7: Bake for 50–60 minutes, checking with a toothpick for doneness. If the top darkens too quickly, tent loosely with foil halfway through; once baked, cool the loaf in the pan for 10 minutes before moving it to a wire rack to cool completely before slicing.

Optional Icing:

If you want a simple finish, whisk together powdered sugar with a little milk and a touch of lemon or orange juice until pourable. Drizzle it over the cooled loaf for a light, sweet citrus topping. This is completely optional, but it pairs beautifully with the buttery crumb and gives extra charm to a gluten-free loaf cake.

Tips For Success:

  • Use softened butter and room-temperature eggs so the batter mixes smoothly and evenly.
  • Cream the butter and sugar long enough for the mixture to become light and fluffy, not just combined.
  • Choose a gluten-free flour blend that contains xanthan gum for the best texture and structure.
  • Mix only until combined once the flour goes in to keep the loaf tender and prevent a dense finish.
  • Let the cake cool completely before slicing so the crumb can fully set and cut cleanly.

Frequently Asked Questions:

Can I make this loaf dairy-free?

Yes. The notes allow for plant-based butter and non-dairy milk, which makes this an easy swap if you need a dairy-free version. Choose options with a mild flavor so the buttery vanilla taste still shines through.

How should I store leftover cake?

You can keep it at room temperature for 2–3 days, refrigerate it for up to 5 days, or freeze it for up to 2 months. Make sure it is well wrapped so the crumb stays moist and does not dry out.

What can I serve with it?

This loaf is delicious plain, but it is also wonderful with fruit, whipped cream, or a citrus glaze. Those simple additions complement the rich crumb without taking away from the classic feel of the cake.
